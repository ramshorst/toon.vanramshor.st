## Context

Oiko is a French real estate management company with a portfolio of buildings and sites rented out primarily to professional tenants. Managing properties at that scale means keeping on top of a large and legally sensitive document set — every asset in the portfolio needs the right documents attached, and those documents need to stay current.

## What I built

I worked as a design engineer on a Nuxt.js and TypeScript project to build their internal management platform. The centrepiece was a document compliance tool structured as a tree: a hierarchy of properties, buildings, and units, each node carrying a set of required documents. The interface let the team see at a glance what was missing, what had expired, and what needed attention.

## The interesting problem

Document compliance in real estate is fundamentally a completeness and freshness problem. The UI had to make a large, nested structure scannable and actionable — surfacing what was missing or out of date without overwhelming the user with the full depth of the tree. Designing that information hierarchy and the status communication around it was the core design challenge.

## Outcome

A focused B2B tool that gave the Oiko team a clear, structured view of their document obligations across their entire portfolio — replacing what was likely a fragmented spreadsheet or manual process with something purpose-built for the way they actually worked.
