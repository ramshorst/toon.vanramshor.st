## Context

What started as a three-month UI contract extended to three years. The company operates in a high-security environment in the semiconductor industry, building internal tooling for managing complex engineering and production processes — covering everything from SoC design to wafer fabrication, dies, and gate-level workflows.

## The problem

The existing platform was large, aging, and increasingly difficult to use and maintain. It had grown organically over time and needed to be rebuilt with usability and maintainability as the primary goals. The challenge was doing that across 100+ highly complex screens without disrupting an active B2B environment.

## What I built

I led the UI design and front-end engineering effort on the rebuild. The work involved user interviews to understand how operators and engineers actually used the system, redesigning screens to improve clarity and reduce friction, and implementing everything in React and TypeScript with Tailwind.

Alongside the product work, I built a design system as a shared NPM package used across two different products — one more customer-facing, one focused on production insights — both drawing on the same component library built with Storybook.

The platform also included PDF generation for production reporting and data visualisation tools for tracking wafer production metrics.

## Working context

This was a fully remote engagement in a high-security environment, requiring careful handling of access, confidentiality, and process. The NDA covers the company name and specific product details.
