## Context

Trackstone was a small-team startup: two people, a clear problem, and a lot of surface area to cover. The product targeted property investors looking for rental yield opportunities in France — taking the manual work out of scanning listings and running the numbers.

## What I built

I designed and developed the full product UI in Vue.js. The application scraped a prominent French property listings platform and enriched that data with government datasets to generate pre-calculated rental yield simulations. Investors could browse properties with the analysis already done, rather than doing the maths themselves.

## The product

The interface was analytics-heavy: data visualisations, filters, simulation outputs, and property detail views — all built around helping someone quickly decide whether a listing was worth pursuing. I also handled email notifications, and the whole product ran on AWS.

## Why it was interesting

Working in a two-person team means every design and engineering decision has immediate impact. There was no abstraction between what we built and what users saw. The combination of scraped data, government data, and financial simulation logic created some genuinely interesting UI problems around how to present uncertainty, surface the right signals, and make a complex model feel simple to use.
