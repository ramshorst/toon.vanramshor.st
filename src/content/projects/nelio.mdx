## Context

Nelio operated a premium food delivery service in Lyon and Paris: high-quality produce sourced and delivered by bike, with multiple pickup points and multi-stop routes. The operation combined the complexity of fresh food logistics with a consumer-facing e-commerce experience.

## What I designed

I designed the UI for the native iPad app used by the food preparation team. The app was the operational heart of the fulfilment workflow — order management, preparation, and handoff — across around 50 pickup points.

## The interesting problem

The core design challenge came from variable-weight products. Customers could order, for example, 800 grams of meat, but the actual prepared weight would differ slightly. The app needed to support a workflow where preparers could weigh the product, enter the real weight, and have the pricing adjust accordingly — all without breaking the flow of a fast-paced kitchen environment.

That made this more than a standard e-commerce management UI. It required thinking carefully about input speed, error recovery, and how to surface the right information at the right moment for someone working quickly with their hands.

## Outcome

The project was a focused UI design engagement — no front-end development on my side, just design. It was a good exercise in designing operational tooling for a physical workflow, where clarity and speed matter more than visual flair.
