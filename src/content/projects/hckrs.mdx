## Context

hckrs.io was a social network for hackers — developers, designers, and makers — built around the idea of local tech communities. The premise was simple: a place to find other people who build things, organised by city, with profiles that actually said something useful about what you know and what you work on.

<Screenshot src="/hckrs-logo.png" alt="Monogram, logo and podcast variant" display="wide" />

## What I built

I designed and built the full product as a solo founder using Meteor.js and PostgreSQL. The platform combined a hacker directory with geographic discovery — a map view showing members in your city — alongside rich profiles covering skills, links, and affiliations.

## Fhacktory

Alongside hckrs.io, I organised 4 hackathons under the name Fhacktory — held in both Paris and Lyon. The events brought the hacker community together in person, turning the network from an online directory into something with a physical presence and real-world energy.

## The invite system

Access was invite-only. Each invite code was bit-encoded directly into the URL — the link itself carried information about who was inviting whom. To spread the network in the physical world, I printed these personalised invite URLs onto business cards using a laser printer, turning each card into a traceable entry point into the network.
