## Context

Upfluence is an influencer marketing platform: a search engine for influencers combined with tools to manage campaigns internally and with clients. When I joined in 2016, the product was already substantial but had been built entirely by developers — there was no designer on the team yet.

## What I did

As the first designer, I came into a product with a lot of existing software and surface area. My main work was to unify the different tools into a consistent, coherent experience — improving usability, visual quality, and interaction patterns across the full suite. I worked as a design engineer, moving between design and TypeScript front-end development, which meant I could own changes from concept through to implementation.

Over time the platform expanded into influencer commerce: promo codes, affiliate-style tracking, and the infrastructure around influencer-driven sales. That brought new product surfaces and continued the challenge of keeping a growing SaaS coherent as it scaled.

## Why it was fulfilling

Being the first designer in a developer-led team is a particular kind of privilege. There is a lot of ground to cover and a lot of leverage in each decision. Taking software that works but has never been touched by a designer and systematically lifting its quality — visually, structurally, in terms of how it feels to use — is deeply satisfying work. It also sharpened my ability to work closely with engineers and earn trust in a context where design was new.
