## Context

Between 2007 and 2012, I built a series of websites for Domaine de l'Amiral, a bed and breakfast. Rather than one generic site, the strategy was to create several targeted websites for different audiences and travel contexts, each with its own positioning and conversion flow.

## What I built

I created multiple PHP/MySQL websites powered by my custom CMS. One version targeted motorbike travellers and included GPS tracks and routes. Another focused on travellers heading toward Spain, with a simplified homepage form that made it easy to request availability and receive confirmation by email. There was also a more general main website for the bed and breakfast itself, including a contact flow and guestbook, and later I also worked on a version aimed at cycling holidays.

## The interaction problem

The interesting problem here was not just building pages, but designing the right entry point for each type of visitor. The most effective version reduced friction by putting an availability request form directly on the homepage, making it fast for travellers to contact the property and easy for the owners to respond.

## Outcome

This work became an important step in learning SEO and acquisition. By combining audience-specific websites, targeted messaging, specialized forum outreach, and early link-building strategies, the project generated strong inquiry volume and showed how tailored web experiences could directly support bookings. → Domaine de l'Amiral
