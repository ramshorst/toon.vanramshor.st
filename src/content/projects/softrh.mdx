## Context

softRH.fr was a galaxy of 26 niche websites, each focused on a specific vertical of B2B SaaS software. The core product was a comparison engine: users could discover, evaluate, and compare SaaS tools in their category. Collectively the network served 35,000 static pages.

## What I built

The entire operation was built and run as a founder. Each site was optimized for search — schema.org structured data, Lighthouse scores of 100/100, page loads under 100ms, and CDN delivery on AWS. The content pipeline was heavily automated: I scraped the homepages of SaaS products to extract descriptions, pricing signals, and feature information, then used word spinning and GPT-3 content generation to produce articles at scale.

Scraping, content generation, and deployments were all scheduled and orchestrated via GitHub Actions. The architecture prioritized static output for performance and SEO — fast pages rank better and cost less to serve.

## The interesting problem

Running 26 websites as a solo founder meant every manual process had to be automated. Keeping 35,000 pages fresh required scheduled scraping runs that would detect changes on product homepages and trigger targeted redeployments. GPT-3 was early and unreliable — working with it in production required significant prompt engineering and output validation to keep content quality acceptable.

Achieving 100/100 Lighthouse scores consistently across a large static site network was an exercise in discipline: image optimization, critical CSS inlining, minimal JavaScript, proper caching headers, and CDN configuration all had to be right across every template.

## Outcome

The network generated organic SEO traffic across its niches through a combination of technical performance and content volume. It was an early experiment in automated content generation and programmatic SEO at scale — years before these became common playbook items.
