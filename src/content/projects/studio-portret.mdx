## Context

<Screenshot src="/studio-portret.png" alt="Studio Portret — portrait photography website" display="wide" />

This started as a promotional website for a portrait photographer, with a strong focus on presenting visual work elegantly online. The original browsing interaction was built around a grid of thumbnails that revealed larger preview images on hover, alongside tabs for contact and supporting information.

## What I built

I designed the interaction model and developed the site in PHP and MySQL. The project later grew into a larger platform where the photographer could upload client sessions, customers could log in to view their photos, and order prints in different sizes, qualities, and colour options. I also added analytics and email notifications for new orders.

## The interaction problem

The key design challenge was balancing portfolio presentation with commercial workflow. The site needed to feel visually polished for showcasing photography, while also becoming a practical tool for session delivery, customer access, and print sales.

## Outcome

What began as a portfolio site evolved into a more complete client gallery and ordering platform. It combined interaction design, e-commerce thinking, and operational tooling in a way that made the website part of the photographer's business workflow.
